Automated account setup for Zenreach

When a merchant signs up for Zenreach, the first thing they need to do is install a WiFi router at their business location. Once the router has been successfully installed, they need to create a dashboard account, log in, design and configure a series of automated email campaigns, social ads and a WiFi portal for contact collection.

Originally, every asset needed to be designed one-by-one using a WYSIWYG composer. We had a hunch that this was a time-consuming and annoying UX. Merchant interviews (and a large number of incomplete signups) seemed to confirm that. Further, we found that even those who were able to complete the process were unhappy with the look-and-feel of their campaigns.

My hypothesis was that idea of merchants designing their own campaigns one-by-one was fundamentally flawed. Small business owners are busy generalists. Most don’t have the time, interest or expertise to devise, design and launch a multi-platform marketing campaign on their own. I wanted to position our merchants as Creative Directors, not Production Designers.

We built a tool that automatically generates a basic color palette using the merchants’ Facebook profile image.
We developed a matrix of default copy to pre-populate a merchant’s campaigns based on their selected brand voice.
SLIDESHOW: Click through the flow on your own. Most users were able to fully configure their account in < 10 minutes.

Dan Nieh and I took on this challenge as part of a 3-day Hackathon. We realized that the easiest way to do this was to have merchants define a set of global parameters (we called it a ‘brand profile’) for their business. We found that we could automatically pull a logo, color palette, photography and location information by simply having the merchant link their Facebook business page. From there, we asked users to choose their best photographs, describe their brand voice and select the minimum and maximum incentives they were willing to offer to bring in customers.

Using this brand profile we were able to generate suggested campaigns which the user could then modify, approve and launch. The suggested campaigns included on-brand accent colors, photography, pre-populated marketing copy and a range of incentives based on the ‘difficulty’ and quality of the audience (e.g. small incentive for new customers, big incentive for lost customers).

Using the suggested campaigns, a merchant could get their account fully up-and-running in under 10 minutes. This feature later became an integral part of Zenreach’s user onboarding process.
